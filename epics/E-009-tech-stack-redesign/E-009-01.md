## Description

After this story is complete, a binding technology decision exists for the baseball-crawl serving layer. The decision record documents which deployment option was chosen (Option A: Native Cloudflare; or Option B: Docker + Cloudflare Access), the evidence from four research spikes that drove the choice, the exact tech stack per layer, and the implementation stories that follow. This story is PM-owned and executed as a decision gate: read research, synthesize, decide, record.

## Context

This is the gate story for E-009. No implementation stories (E-009-02 through E-009-08) are written or dispatched until this story is DONE. The research phase (R-01 through R-04) is complete. Four research spikes evaluated Option A and Option B across database, API, dashboard, and infrastructure dimensions.

**Outcome**: All four research spikes recommended Option B (Docker + Cloudflare Access). The PM reviewed the consensus, evaluated the decision criteria from R-04, and confirmed Option B. The decision record is recorded at `/.project/decisions/E-009-decision.md`.

## Technical Approach

The PM reads all four research notes, applies the decision criteria from R-04 (five priority-ranking questions), and synthesizes a decision. The decision is recorded as a markdown file. No code is written. No tools beyond Read and Write are required.

**Decision criteria applied (from R-04)**:

1. Is zero operational overhead worth 2-4 weeks of TypeScript learning?
2. Is vendor lock-in acceptable for infrastructure savings?
3. Will baseball-crawl need new developers? What language would they know?
4. Is SSH observability and log inspection valuable?
5. Is the ability to migrate away from Cloudflare important?

If Option B answers 3-5 questions, choose Option B. If Option A answers 3-5, choose Option A.
